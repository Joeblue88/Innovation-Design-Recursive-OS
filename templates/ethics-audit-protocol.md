# Ethics Audit Protocol

A gating checklist to run before any research, prototyping, or technology implementation phase. Ensures that solutions serve human agency and social stewardship.

## 1. Intent & Framing
- What human need or tension does this solution address?
- Whose problem is this *not*? Who is being designed *around* rather than *for*?
- Have we converted any "How do we sell X?" framings into "How do we empower X?"

## 2. Data Stewardship
- What data is collected, and is each field strictly necessary?
- Is consent informed, granular, and revocable?
- Where is data stored, who can access it, and for how long?
- Could this data be misused if leaked or repurposed?

## 3. Inclusion & Exclusion
- Who benefits most from this solution?
- Who is excluded — by language, ability, geography, income, or device?
- Have we tested with users from at least three distinct contexts?

## 4. Power & Agency
- Does this solution increase or decrease user agency?
- Are users able to opt out, export their data, or appeal automated decisions?
- Does it concentrate power in a single platform, vendor, or operator?

## 5. Long-Term Societal Impact
- If this solution succeeded at 100x scale, what second-order effects would emerge?
- What jobs, behaviors, or institutions would it displace?
- Would we be comfortable with this scaled outcome on a public front page?

## 6. Decision
- [ ] **Proceed** — Risks identified and mitigated.
- [ ] **Proceed with conditions** — List required mitigations:
- [ ] **Pause** — Issues require rework before continuing.
- [ ] **Stop** — Ethical risk outweighs value.
